Research & Innovation

Projects

An overview of AIDIRL's active research and development projects, spanning low-resource language resources, multilingual AI evaluation, agentic AI systems, and edge deployment of large language models.

Flagship programme: low-resource language & agentic AI

Ongoing

BNer

The first gold-standard Named Entity Recognition dataset for Balochi — 58,893 tokens across five domains, used to benchmark CRF, SVM, and KNN approaches.

Ongoing

BGLUE

A GLUE/SuperGLUE-style general language-understanding evaluation benchmark for Balochi, providing standardised metrics for cross-model comparison on this critically under-resourced language.

Ongoing

BIT

An instruction-tuning dataset for Balochi, enabling supervised fine-tuning of large language models for instruction-following, chat, and task-completion.

Ongoing

Gemma for Pakistani Languages

Fine-tuning and edge deployment of Gemma for Balochi, Urdu, Sindhi, Pashto, Punjabi, Saraiki, and Brahui — extended with multimodal text, audio, and visual capabilities for resource-constrained devices.

Ongoing

TFAgent

An agentic AI system that automates tax-return filing for Pakistani taxpayers through the Federal Board of Revenue portal.

Ongoing

NavAgent

A multilingual, multimodal mobile navigation agent delivering turn-by-turn directions and contextual assistance across Pakistani languages.

Submitted to NeurIPS

Global PIQA — Balochi Component

Contribution of the Balochi-language component to Global PIQA, a participatory commonsense-reasoning benchmark spanning 140+ language varieties, built by 350+ researchers.

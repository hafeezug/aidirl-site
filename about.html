About the Lab

Purpose, scope, and governance

AIDIRL is proposed as a specialised, advanced-capability research facility within the Department of Computer Science, dedicated to high-quality applied research in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data science.

Purpose & Scope

The Lab occupies the research frontier of the Department's laboratory ecosystem — distinct from its teaching-oriented laboratories — providing the computational infrastructure, curated datasets, and collaborative environment needed to produce original contributions to knowledge and translate research insight into deployable solutions.

Its scope spans the full research pipeline: data acquisition and curation, exploratory analysis, model development, experimental evaluation, and dissemination of findings, supporting independent faculty research alongside postgraduate and advanced undergraduate supervision.

Vision & Mission

Vision: to act as a regional research hub that translates national AI priorities into locally grounded research addressing the developmental challenges of Gwadar and Balochistan, while strengthening the Department's standing in the national higher-education sector.

Mission: to advance original, problem-driven AI and data-science research — aligned with priorities identified by federal ministries and industry — and to build the datasets, talent, and partnerships needed to sustain it.

Strategic Objectives

  • Advance original research in AI, machine learning, NLP, computer vision, and data analytics relevant to regional, national, and global development priorities.
  • Conduct problem-driven, Pakistan-specific research aligned with federal ministry and private-sector priorities, including national AI research competitions.
  • Support capstone projects, postgraduate theses, and faculty-led research across healthcare informatics, precision agriculture, marine resource management, smart infrastructure, and fintech.
  • Undertake systematic development, curation, and public dissemination of high-quality datasets addressing Pakistan's social, economic, and governance challenges.
  • Foster research-led innovation and entrepreneurship, enabling functional prototypes and commercialisable research outputs.
  • Establish sustained collaborative relationships with peer institutions and external organisations.
  • Secure sustained research funding from national and international sources.
  • Lead a flagship research programme in low-resource language AI for under-represented Pakistani languages.

Core Research & Innovation Activities

  • Faculty-led research projects, including externally grant-funded initiatives.
  • Student research at undergraduate and postgraduate levels — capstone projects, Masters dissertations, and doctoral research.
  • Joint collaborative projects with universities, government agencies, NGOs, and industry.
  • A regular programme of research seminars, reading groups, guest lectures, and technical workshops.
  • Innovation incubation — hackathons, AI challenge competitions, and proof-of-concept development sprints.

Core Infrastructure & Facilities

  • High-performance workstations and dedicated GPU servers for training, fine-tuning, and deploying deep learning models.
  • Secure, access-controlled storage for curated research datasets spanning text, image, speech, time-series, and tabular data.
  • A comprehensive AI and data-science software stack — PyTorch, TensorFlow, scikit-learn, Hugging Face Transformers, Apache Spark, and supporting tools.
  • Collaborative workspace with flexible seating, presentation facilities, and high-bandwidth connectivity for hybrid seminars.
  • Access to cloud computing resources (AWS, Google Cloud, or equivalent) for computationally intensive workloads.
  • A dedicated data-annotation workstation suite with quality-assurance workflows.

National Dataset Development Initiative

Pakistan faces a marked shortage of high-quality, standardised datasets for locally relevant problems. AIDIRL undertakes systematic data collection, curation, annotation, and publication — using online platforms and physical sensing infrastructure such as IoT sensors — adopting international standards for dataset construction.

All datasets are created with appropriate anonymisation and ethical safeguards, with partnerships pursued to responsibly access validated administrative datasets supporting the Lab's priorities in governance, healthcare, agriculture, and marine resource management.

Research Funding & Sustainability

Nationally, the Government of Pakistan's National Artificial Intelligence Fund (NAIF) and the AI-designated allocation within the Ignite Fund represent primary funding avenues, alongside Higher Education Commission programmes — including the National Research Program for Universities (NRPU), the Technology Development Fund (TDF), and the Problem-Based Applied Interdisciplinary Research Program (PBAIRP) — and Pakistan Science Foundation grants.

Internationally, the Lab pursues funding from multilateral and bilateral agencies (e.g. World Bank, UNDP, UNICEF), research foundations, and philanthropic organisations, alongside research consultancy and industry-sponsored partnerships.

Governance, Ethics & Responsible AI

  • All research activities are subject to institutional ethical review and data-protection compliance assessment, with particular rigour for sensitive, personal, or proprietary data.
  • Students and staff receive structured guidance in responsible AI principles — algorithmic fairness, model transparency, accountability, data privacy, and societal implications.
  • The Lab encourages open dissemination of research findings in peer-reviewed venues, with appropriate recognition of contributors, collaborators, and funding bodies.

Expected Outcomes & Anticipated Impact

  • Strengthened institutional capacity for cutting-edge AI, machine learning, and data-science research at the University of Gwadar.
  • Creation and public contribution of standardised datasets addressing Pakistan's social, economic, and governance challenges.
  • Stronger capacity to attract externally funded research projects and strategic partnerships.
  • Enhanced collaboration between the University, government ministries, industry, and international organisations.
  • Meaningful contribution to regional development, including marine resource management and smart coastal-city infrastructure.
  • Recognition of the Department of Computer Science as a centre of excellence in applied AI research within Pakistan.

Collaborate With Us

AIDIRL welcomes conversations with universities, government agencies, NGOs, and industry partners interested in joint research, dataset collaboration, or student supervision.
